IQ TRIVIA

Team Size: 4

Event Description

IQ Trivia Quiz comprises of two rounds.

First Round (Qualifier):

This round consisted of 30 questions with a 30-minute time limit. Teams were ranked based on their scores, and the highest-scoring teams qualified for the second round.

Second Round:

This round was comprised of four levels. The team with the highest overall score across all four levels was declared the winner.

Round Levels

Level 1: General

  • Each team will be asked 3 questions of 10 marks each. they will be given 30 seconds for each question.
  • If the allotted team is unable to answer the question, then the question will pass on to the subsequent teams.
  • Subsequent team will be given 15 seconds to answer & will be awarded 5 marks for each correct answer.

Level 2: Buzzer round

  • There will be 5 questions in this round. Team pressing the buzzer first will be given the chance to answer.
  • 10 marks will be awarded for each correct answer & 5 marks will be deducted for each incorrect answer.
  • Time limit: 60 seconds for each question.

Level 3: Audio-Visual round

  • Teams will answer questions about an image, audio or video clip played for them.
  • Each correct answer is worth 15 points.
  • Time limit: 60 Seconds for each question.

Level 4: Rapid fire Round

  • One designated member from each team will answer the questions.
  • Only that nominated member may provide answers.
  • Each correct answer is worth 20 marks.
  • Time limit: 90 seconds for 10 questions.

Rules

Replacement of a team member is not allowed after registration.

Each correct answer is worth points.

Correct spelling is not required; however, answers must be legible.

No yelling out answers, right or wrong. It disrupts the game and can bother other players.

There are four rounds.

We'll take a break for 10 minutes after round two.

No outside sources allowed.

No cell phone use is allowed.

If any team found using gadgets team will be declared as disqualified.

In the event of a tie, a tie-breaker question will be asked. The team that answers that question correctly will be the winner.

When we ask a question, we've researched it. The answer we have is the correct answer.

The decision of the host is final and will not be subject to change.
